## Results

Thrust (T): MAE = 0.60 N, RMSE = 0.66 N, NRMSE = 6.6%

Power (P): MAE = 0.71 W, RMSE = 0.84 W, NRMSE = 1.1%

The results indicate a good agreement between the BEMT predictions and the experimental data reported by Horn (2019). For thrust, the mean absolute error is 0.60 N and the root mean square error is 0.66 N, corresponding to only 6.6% of the total variation observed in the measurements. This shows that the discrepancies are small and well distributed over the operating range.

For power, the agreement is even stronger: the mean absolute error is 0.71 W and the RMSE is 0.84 W, which represents just 1.1% of the measured range. This level of accuracy indicates that the torque and power predictions provided by the BEMT model are consistent with the experimental trends.

Overall, the model demonstrates a good level of fidelity in reproducing the experimental performance of the propeller, confirming that the adopted calibration and aerodynamic modeling approach lead to a reliable prediction of both thrust and power across the tested rotational speeds.
